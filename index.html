Introduction about Smishing

The Philippines, known as the text capital of the world with over 83 million mobile phone users, has also become a hotspot for smishing, or SMS phishing. Smishing attacks have taken advantage of the widespread use of mobile phones and texting as a primary communication method, leading to cybercriminals deceiving people through misleading text messages to gain sensitive information or install malware. As senior high school students, there is a need to comprehend the risks associated with smishing and take steps to protect ourselves and our communities. Smishing has become a global issue, with millions of people falling victim to these attacks annually. Cybercriminals often impersonate trustworthy organizations, making it challenging for individuals to differentiate between legitimate messages and smishing attempts. In our local community, we have witnessed the detrimental effects of smishing attacks, with many people receiving suspicious texts and some falling prey to scams. Consequently, it is crucial to raise awareness and educate individuals about smishing to mitigate its prevalence. To address this escalating threat, our research aims to determine the most effective approaches for increasing awareness and educating individuals about smishing attacks, ultimately reducing their occurrence in our community.
